A third place to skate

    A third place is a location where people can gather that isn't their home (first place) or workplace (second place).

    It's difficult to find third places that aren't:
    A) Expensive.
    B) Commercialised.
    C) Seasonal.

    Skateland is:
    A) Cheap (3 with your own skates, 5 to hire skates).
    B) Primarily run by volunteers.
    C) Open all year round.

    There's a tuck shop that reminds me of a school disco. The prices don't sting and the drinks won't get you drunk.

    Some skate backwards, some cling to the railings the whole way around. I don't see people laugh at each other, only with each other.

    I'm too empathetic (and slow) to capture a photo of anyone falling over.

    Many hone their routines and tricks, but the biggest challenge is navigating a wet environment where precise aim is required.

    Skateland is largely run by volunteer stewards like Dwayne and Charlotte.

    Dwayne's been coming to Skateland for seven years. He tells me that it's a great way to connect with his daughters. His voice echos out over the rink as none of the other stewards are comfortable using the PA system. He jokes that he's the worst man for the job due to his speech impediment.

    Dwayne doesn't just rink skate, there are different skates for different activities. Bigger wheels for uneven outdoor surfaces and chunkier bars for doing tricks.

    Charlotte started skating again in her 40s. Her day job is in finance. She got back into it after attending a pop-up roller rink in the Victoria Centre during a Covid summer.

    Like Dwayne, she was asked to become a steward once she'd been a regular at Skateland for several months.

    There's always another photographer. Like car people, cyclists, and other hobbyists; they can't resist the magnetic pull of gear and gadgets.

    I feel ill-prepared to talk to them. I'm letting them down with my lack of knowledge. But they don't need you to know what you're talking about if you're willing to have a conversation. I listened to Jacob suggest some exposure settings I should try out, and I tried them.

    As the night goes on, the lights get dimmer and the rink gets more crowded. New arrivals are greeted by groups of familiar faces, friends, and fellow skaters.


    This isn't the final stop of the night. When Skateland closes they'll be out skating in the empty car parks of deserted retail parks. Their skates the dynamos generating the third space.
